User Guide: Topic Visualization with LDAvis
  • View your LDAvis Report.
  • What is LDAvis? It's an interactive visual tool for understanding the topics generated by LDA (Latent Dirichlet Allocation). Each bubble represents a topic.
  • Bubble Position: The center and position of each bubble on the left panel shows the inter-topic distance — how distinct or similar topics are from one another. Overlapping bubbles mean related topics.
  • Bubble Size: The size of a bubble indicates the overall prevalence of the topic across your document set (larger = more common).
  • Right Panel (Bar Chart): When you click a topic on the left, the right panel shows the top terms that define that topic. - Red bars = term frequency within the topic. - Gray bars = frequency across all topics (relevance comparison).
  • Lambda (λ) Slider: Adjusting λ lets you change how specific vs. general the displayed terms are: - λ → 1.0: Shows the most frequent terms in the topic - λ → 0.0: Shows more exclusive terms (more useful for topic interpretation)
  • Hover Tips: Hovering over bars or bubbles shows tooltips for deeper understanding — including term probabilities and relevance.
  • Why Use This? To visually explore how well your LDA model groups topics and which keywords best represent each one. It helps validate the quality and coherence of your model.

User Guide
  • What-type Questions: These are factual or descriptive questions aimed at understanding the basic definition or explanation of a concept or technology.
    Example: "What is REST API?"
    Insight: High volume indicates demand for foundational knowledge, common in newer or complex domains.
  • How-type Questions: These questions ask about implementation steps, configurations, or practical usage of tools and technologies.
    Example: "How to send data over MQTT?"
    Insight: Reflects hands-on learning needs and technical challenge in applying the concept.
  • Why-type Questions: These involve reasoning, justification, or conceptual clarity about design choices or technology preferences.
    Example: "Why is REST preferred over SOAP?"
    Insight: Indicates users seeking deeper understanding; often appears in mature or evaluative discussions.
  • Other-type Questions: Covers all remaining types such as tool recommendations, vague queries, error troubleshooting, or mixed-context issues.
    Example: "Facing error 404 in Flask app"
    Insight: Captures practical or non-structured challenges, often indicating a need for better support or clarification.
